Where can you find corals?

Corals are found in deep and shallow water. They are mostly located around the Pacific Ocean, Tropics of Cancer, Indian Ocean, The Red Sea, and the Caribbean Sea.

Read More

What is a coral?

Corals are not Rocks or Plants Coral’s are colonial organism, made up of hundreds of flower-like individual animals, called polyps.

Read More

what can corals do?

They act as barriers for coastlines by breaking up the power of waves, provide jobs for local communities, and offer opportunities for recreation. They are also a source of new medicines and food.

Read More

What is a natural threat to the coral reefs?

Waves can break coral fragments loose. Unusually long periods of low tides can overheat and dry out shallow corals. Extended rainfall can also reduce the salinity of shallow areas. Coral’s soft inner tissue are vulnerable to predation by fish.

Read More

Why are reefs Important?

Coral reefs are the lungs of the oceans. It means they are absorbing co2 from the atmosphere and transforming it into oxygen, we breathe. They fight climate change by regulating air and water temperature for us all. Without coral reefs, there will be no ocean and no air.

Read More

How do we impact coral reefs?

We are a major threat to coral reefs. Pollution (leaking fuels, plastic pollution, oil spill , pesticides etc.), is a big problem. Deep water trawling where fishing net is dragged along the ocean floor can kill corals. Warming climate is a major threat, increased by human activity.

Read More

Can we help?

By the year 2050 all coral reefs may be dead, which will affect hundreds of millions of people and the planet. By replanting coral cuttings onto degraded areas of reef, the fragments will grow and help to recreate reef habitats. This will let other life return and give strength to the reef against damage in the future. You can help make a change. Adopt a coral today

Where can you find corals?

Some people might think that coral reefs only can be found in tropical areas, close to the surface. However, this is not the case. Coral reefs can also be found in cold and deep waters.

The reefs found in cold and deep waters are just made by different species, than the reefs that are growing in warmer and shallower waters. So coral reefs can almost be found all around the world.

The largest coral reef can be found by the northern part of Australia’s east coast. This reef is called Great Barrier Reef and contains 2600 induvial reefs that spread over 2000 kilometers.

What is a coral and what can corals do?

A coral is made by organisms called coral polyps. One coral is most often made up by made many polyps that live together.

Coral polyps have the shape of a cylinder. They have a mouth that have tentacles around it, that helps the organism gather food. The polyps are soft until they settle on a stone on the reef, then they begin to use the ions that are in the seawater, to make a limestone exoskeleton.

When you think of corals, you might think of them as really colorful. Which is not wrong, but only some corals are colored. A lot of corals are actually more of a brown color. The corals get their colors from algae that are growing on them.

What is a natural threat to the coral reefs?

Climate change is one of threats that coral reefs are being exposed to right now. Corals can´t survive if the waters temperature keeps rising. Even a small increase in temperature is deadly for the corals. Climate change has already been the cause of severe bleaching – which means the corals turn white and dies. According to scientist, if the temperature keeps rising at this pace, most of the coral reefs could very well be gone in the next few decades.

Overfishing is another threat to the coral reefs. It is a threat, because if humans catch too many of the fish living on the reef, the entire ecosystem is affected. On healthy coral reefs the fish keep algae growth low. Without the fish keeping the algae down, the coral reefs will end up smothered by the algae.

What is a natural threat to the coral reefs?

Crown-of-thorns which is a starfish that feeds on the corals. Because of the overfishing, the natural threat to the starfish, The Giant Triton, isn’t there to keep the numbers of Crown-of-thorns down. This leads to an increased number of the starfish, that but even more stress on an already challenged coral reef.

Pollution is also a threat to the coral reefs. Plastic, chemicals and oil pollution is a few of the things that are poisoning the coral reefs. Pollution from farming is another thing that is doing great harm to the coral reefs, because of the increase of nitrogen in the water, which lead to the algae growing faster.

How do we impact coral reefs?

The coral reefs are home for many fish and other marine animals, that are really important for humans. It is believed that around 1 billion people are in some way dependent on the coral reefs for food

The coral reefs do also protect land against storms and tsunamis. That is because the reefs can dissolve the energy from the waves.
